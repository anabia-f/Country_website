A Taste of Jamaica


Ackee and Saltfish is Jamaica's national dish, a savory combination of salted codfish and ackee, a unique fruit with a creamy texture. The dish is typically sautéed with onions, tomatoes, scallions, and peppers, creating a flavorful plate. The ackee absorbs the codfish, giving it a buttery taste. The dish is typically served with fried dumplings, boiled green bananas, or festival, it's a popular breakfast or anytime meal that captures the essence of Jamaican cuisine.

Oxtail is a flavorful Jamaican dish made from tender, slow-cooked oxtail simmered in a savory stew. The meat is cooked until it’s fall-off-the-bone tender, with a rich broth made from a blend of spices, herbs, and seasonings like thyme, pimento, garlic, and scotch bonnet peppers. The dish often includes carrots, onions, and butter beans, adding sweetness and texture to the savory stew. Oxtail is typically served with rice and peas or fried dumplings which makes a satisfying meal that’s a favorite in Jamaican homes and restaurants.

Fish Escovitch is a popular dish featuring fried fish (typically snapper or parrotfish) topped with a tangy, flavorful pickled vegetable sauce. The fish is lightly fried until crispy, then smothered in a spicy escovitch sauce made with vinegar, onions, carrots, bell peppers, and scotch bonnet peppers. The sauce is seasoned with garlic, thyme, and pimentio, giving it a perfect balance of heat and sweetness. Often served with fried dumplings, festival, or rice and peas, Fish Escovitch is a vibrant, delicious dish that’s refreshing.

Brown Stew Chicken is a beloved dish where chicken is seasoned with a blend of spices, then browned in oil to create a rich, golden color. The chicken is simmered in a savory sauce made from onions, tomatoes, garlic, bell peppers, thyme, and scallions, with the addition of soy sauce, ketchup, and sometimes a hint of browning sauce for depth and sweetness. The result is tender, flavorful chicken in a rich, slightly sweet and savory gravy. Typically served with rice and peas or fried dumplings, Brown Stew Chicken is a comforting and hearty meal that captures the essence of Jamaican home cooking.

Pepper Pot Soup is a Jamaican dish known for its bold flavors and comforting qualities. This rich and spicy soup is made with tender chunks of beef (often oxtail or stew beef) slow-cooked in a flavorful broth seasoned with Scotch bonnet peppers, thyme, scallions, and allspice. The soup also features callaloo (a leafy green), okra, and pimento seeds, giving it a hearty texture and depth. The addition of spices and hot peppers gives it a distinctive kick, making it perfect for a cool day or as a remedy for a cold. Often served with a side of dumplings or hard dough bread, Pepper Pot Soup is a true taste of the culinary heritage.

Curry Goat is a flavorful dish made with tender goat meat simmered in a rich, spiced curry sauce. The meat is marinated with a blend of curry powder, garlic, ginger, thyme, scallions, and Scotch bonnet peppers, giving it a bold spicy flavor. It's slow-cooked until the goat meat is tender and the curry sauce is thick and savory. The dish is typically served with rice and peas or plain rice, and it's a favorite for special occasions, celebrations, and family gatherings. The combination of spices and tender meat makes Curry Goat a beloved, hearty meal that’s a staple in Jamaican cuisine.

Jerk Chicken is one of Jamaica's most famous dishes, known for its bold, smoky flavor and spicy kick. The chicken is marinated in a flavorful jerk seasoning made from a mix of spices like pimento, thyme, garlic, scallions, ginger, and the fiery Scotch bonnet pepper. The marinade is typically applied overnight to allow the flavors to deeply infuse the meat. Traditionally, Jerk Chicken is cooked over pimento wood or on a grill, which gives it a distinct smoky aroma. The result is perfectly tender chicken with a crispy, charred exterior and a balance of spicy, savory, and slightly sweet flavors. Often served with rice and peas, festival, or bammy, Jerk Chicken is a flavorful and iconic taste of Jamaican cuisine.

Run Down Stew (also known as Run Down or Run Down Fish) is a traditional dish that features fish cooked in a rich, flavorful coconut milk-based stew. The fish, often mackerel or snapper, is simmered in a creamy sauce made from coconut milk, along with onions, tomatoes, scallions, garlic, thyme, and scotch bonnet peppers, giving the dish a spicy and aromatic kick. The addition of ginger and pimento enhances the depth of flavor. The stew is typically served with rice, dumplings, or boiled green bananas. The creamy, coconutty sauce absorbs the natural flavors of the fish, creating a comforting meal that’s loved for its simplicity and bold taste.
